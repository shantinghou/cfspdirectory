$73 vs. $57 RETAINED IN THE LOCAL ECONOMY

For every $100 spent on a local business, $73 stays in the local economy. In comparison, spending $100 at a chain store results in $57 staying in the local economy. The remaining $73 is then dispersed locally in the form of wages, charitable donations, taxes which fund city services, and purchases of goods and services from other local businesses.

Additional consumer spending strengthens local economies.

30% OF AMERICAN BUSINESSES ARE MINORITY OWNED

These businesses employ more than 7 million Americans. Supporting minority businesses helps recerse the effects of corporate discrimination. Shopping at local businesses creates more local jobs than shopping at major chain or online companies. Local businesses not only pay their employees, they also spend money at other local businesses.

91% OF LOCAL BUSINESSES OWNERS CONTRIBUTE TO THEIR COMMUNITY

In a case study of the economic impact of locally owned businesses on the local economy in the Mid-coast Maine region conducted by the Institute for Local Self-Reliance, the charitable contributions made by local businesses were compared to those made by a chain retailer, Wal-Mart, in 2002. For every $1,000,000 in sales, one local business alone contributed $4,000 to Wal-Mart’s $1,000 contribution.
